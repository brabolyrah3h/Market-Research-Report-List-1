<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for the semiconductor market is produced primarily through two processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. CVD involves chemically reacting gaseous precursors to form a solid quartz layer, enabling precise control over thickness and purity, vital for semiconductor applications. Plasma Deposition utilizes ionized gas to deposit quartz layers on substrates, enhancing material properties and uniformity. Both methods are crucial for creating high-quality, high-purity synthetic quartz required in advanced semiconductor manufacturing, ensuring optimal performance and reliability.</p></p>
