<p><p>Prostate cancer nuclear medicine diagnostics primarily utilize SPECT (Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography) and PET (Positron Emission Tomography) technologies. SPECT employs gamma rays to create detailed 3D images, helping to detect bone metastases and evaluate treatment responses. In contrast, PET utilizes radioactive tracers that highlight metabolic activity within cells, enabling the identification of cancerous tissues at an early stage. Both modalities enhance the accuracy of diagnosis and treatment planning, crucial for effective prostate cancer management.</p></p>
